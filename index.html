Introduction

Contextual inquiry

Contextual inquiry is a type of field study that involves in-depth observation and interviews of a small sample of users to gain a robust understanding of work practices and behaviors. For this project, I observed and interview people while they were interacting with an interface. I selected an interface in public view and observed others that would not make them uncomfortable. For this assingment, I chose to write about the Yokai hot soup machine in the RISD portfolio dining hall.

Part 1: Preparation

Accessible interface

The accessible interface I choose was the YoKai hot noodle soup machine located in the RISD portfolio dining hall. I choose this machine because it is a complex mechanism, but has a sleek user interface and I wanted to see how RISD students interacted with this unique machine.

Instructions on how to use Yokai machine

Steps:

  1. Tap the large screen on the left
  2. Read an consent to the terms to proceed with order
  3. Select a bowl
  4. Anwer the Chef
  5. Select payment method
    • Acceptable form of payment are RISD buck or credit cards
  6. Swipe card
  7. Press green arrow
  8. Remember your order number
    • System can process two orders at a time
  9. Wait for door to open
  10. Carefully remove your bowl

Description of interface

The Yokai machine serves different types of hot ramen in 45 seconds. The problem the interface is trying to solve is the ability to order and receive hot food within seconds. Through this interactive interface, users are able to select a variety of bowls and payment options to have a hot meal delivered in 45 seconds. A major key component of the interface is the large touchscreen on the left hand side. This screen provides the main functionality where users can order items and select their payment type. To use the device, a user must accept terms and agreements before scrolling through a list of different types of bowls they can order. Once a user finds a bowl they like, the user can select the bowl to get more information (ie. ingredients and allergies). Finally, the user can select their preferred method of payment and wait for their order to arrive. This process usually takes up to 45 seconds.

Part 2: Recording Observations

I observed 3 people using the Yokai interface and paid careful attention to how they use the interface by making objective observations about their actions. In particular, I observed for any struggles or difficulties that they encounter while using the interface.

Interview questions

  1. Do you think this interface is easier than talking to a waiter?
  2. If you value quick and easy food, why do you prefer this interface over using delivery services like doordash, snackpass or ubereats?
  3. What frustrations have you experienced using this large touchscreen?
  4. Are there more features that you would like the machine to have?
  5. How do you normally pay for Yokai at the machine? Why?

Key Observations

  • There was a sheet of paper on the side of machine to describe exactly how to use it, implying that people in the past had trouble using it
  • A couple of new users were quite confused and took a long time to scroll through all the options and figure out which items they wanted
  • For most people, it usually took less than 5 minutes to figure out their order and receive their bowl
  • Majority of the users ate alone or took their meal away from the main dining hall
  • Very few people used the machine in large groups
  • Some people complained that the machine ran out of the dish that they wanted, but eventually they settled on a dish anyways
  • The food was extremely hot and a lot of users used their jackets to carry the bowls
  • Majority of people either used their RISD bucks or mobile pay (ie. apple pay), very few people used a physical credit card
  • A couple of users quickly tapped the buttons very efficiently and were able to get their order in about 2 minutes.

Summarized responses

  1. Users prefer using the Yokai interface because their main goal is to quickly get decent food and adding a human component could slow down the process. Because there is no human interaction, users are able to reliably obtain food from the Yokai machine.
  2. Users who value a quick meal enjoy using the Yokai machine because it can provide food 24/7, making it extremely convenient for those who flippantly decide that they want food.
  3. Because there are 2 very large touch screens, a lot of people are confused on which screen to order from. The screen is a really nice sleek design, but oftentimes it takes a while to register the user's touch. Thus, it might be nice to have tactile buttons in the scenario in which the user can’t use the touch screen.
  4. A lot of users wished that they could customize their dish with certain ingredients from a preset list and set the water temperature to a specific heat. Oftentimes the bowls come with ingredients that certain people do not like and the temperature of the bowl is way too hot.
  5. Majority of the people who use the Yokai machine either use RISD bucks or Apple pay. Because students have loaded money on their ID cards from their dining hall credits, they find it super convenient to use those credits.

Part 3: Personas

I constructed 2 personas by creating a four-quadrant empathy map, describing what users think, feel, say, and do. These personas represent part of the spectrum of users I observed.

Andy

  • Description

    Andy is an extremely exhausted Architecture student that just finished a 6 hour studio and just wants a quick way to satisfy his hunger.

  • Interface Problems

    A problem that Andy faces with the interface is that sometimes the device doesn’t have the dish that he wants and there is no notification system to alert the service people to restock the machine. Another problem Andy faces is that the interface is not always responsive to his touch and it takes a couple of tries for him for the buttons to register his touch. Andy is also unable to customize some of the ingredients in his dish and is unable to remove mushrooms from his bowl.

  • Persona Representation

    This persona represents a large portion of students that use the machine during the middle of the day and have the option of going to one of the dining halls instead. A lot of RISD students are exhausted after their long studios and want to eat something as soon as possible. This persona embodies those who value a quick meal rather than a freshly prepared meal by RISD chefs.

Alice

  • Description

    Alice is an Industrial Design student who was staying up late to finish up an intense semester long project and wants to get some quick and convenient food before continuing to work on her project.

  • Interface Problems

    A problem that Alice faces is that the interface does not allow her to set the temperature of the ramen, which is automatically set to boiling. Although the machine is extremely fast at making the food, Alice wishes that there was an option to order online beforehand so she can walk up to the machine from her dorm and quickly grab her meal.

  • Persona Representation

    This persona represents a large portion of students that use the machine while dining halls are not operating. Because this machine is located inside one of RISD’s dorms, a lot of students like using it as a quick and convenient meal. Thus, when it is late at night and students want a quick meal before continuing their work, the Yokai machine satisfies their hunger. This persona embodies those who value an easy meal rather than making a midnight snack of their own.

Part 4: Storyboard

A storyboard represents my persona’s user journey from start to end. It reflects the goals of my personas and includse all necessary steps for using an interface where the entire user journey is depicted. Under each frame, a caption of what’s happening is provided.

Andy just finished a 6 Architecture hour studio. He is exhausted and starving because he hasn't eaten anything since 8:00 am.

When Andy arrived at the portfolio dinning hall, he was met with a long line of fellow classmates who were also similarly hungry.

Because Andy was starving and just wanted some food to satisfy his hunger, he decided to use the Yokai machine.

Andy walked up to the machine and started ordering his food. The first screen displayed a disclaimer about how hot the food can be.

Andy used the large touch screen on the left to browse through all the various hot bowl options. A lot of bowls were all sold out.

Andy settled on the "Tokyo Shoyu Ramen" even though it is not his favorite dish. Andy is just starving and wants something to eat.

After Andy selected his dish, a menu asking for his preferred method of payment popped up. He has the choice of RISD bucks, credit cards or mobile payment.

Andy has a lot of RISD bucks and so he decided to use his RISD ID to pay for his food.

Only 45 seconds after Andy placed his order, his meal was ready and was delivered in one of the dispensers.

While everyone else was still waiting in line for the portfolio food, Andy happily already started eating his ramen from the Yokai machine.
